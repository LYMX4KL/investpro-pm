Rental Application

Application Fee: $75 primary applicant, $50 each additional adult. Every adult living in the home must submit their own application. A Holding Fee is also collected with every application — refundable if you're denied; forfeited if you're approved but don't sign the lease.

What happens after you apply: (1) Submit your application + pay both fees. (2) Admin auto-sends Verification of Rental History (VOR) to your prior landlord and Employment Verification to your employer. (3) Credit + background + pet screening run via our partners. (4) PM (and owner, when required) reviews and decides. (5) If approved, term sheet emailed for acceptance. (6) Lease signing, orientation, move-in. Already applied? Check status.

7 · Pets
Service animals, ESAs, and assistance animals are NOT pets. Per CFR / NRS / Fair Housing Act, those animals require a separate Lease Addendum for Service and/or Emotional Support Animals — not this section. If you have one, complete that addendum (we'll email you the link after you submit). All other animals — even small pets — go here.

Pet screening: All pets must be registered through PetScreening.com as part of our review. We'll send you the link after you submit. Some breeds are restricted by our insurance — check the Unacceptable Breeds list before applying with a dog (we'll email it with your confirmation).

                12 · Holding Fee Agreement

                A Holding Fee is collected with every application. It "holds" the property for you while we review.

                How the Holding Fee works:
                • If your application is denied → the Holding Fee is refunded to you in full within the timeframe set by Nevada law (typically 7 business days).
                • If you are approved but choose not to sign the lease → the Holding Fee is forfeited as compensation for the property being held off the market during your decision.
                • If you sign the lease → the Holding Fee is applied toward your security deposit or first month's rent (specified in your term sheet).
                Typically equal to one month's rent. Final amount confirmed by your property manager.
                Time you have after approval to sign the lease and pay deposit.
